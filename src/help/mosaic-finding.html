Mosaic - Finding Planetary Images

The Scientist or Planetary Enthusiast is expected to have their map-projected orbital images locally available for use by POMM as an outgrowth of their study or hobby. However, if data are needed, this section provides an introduction to finding an area-of-interest and obtaining the associated orbital imagery. Note that online map-projected images tend to be less available than raw/EDR PDS-formatted images. The links below support mission data supported by POMM map-projection tools (i.e., CTX, HiRISE, HRSC, LRO NAC/WAC).

  • Finding a Mars or Luna/Moon Area-of-Interest (AOI):
    • Mars Trek: https://trek.nasa.gov/mars/
      • Find interesting areas on Mars. Record your AOI's Latitude/Longitude coordinates and Mars feature name.
    • Moon Trek: https://trek.nasa.gov/moon/
      • Find interesting areas on Earth's Moon, like the Apollo Landing sites. Record your AOI's Latitude/Longitude coordinates and Moon feature name.
    • I-Mars GIS: http://i-mars.eu/web-gis/
      • Find interesting areas on Mars. Record your AOI's Latitude/Longitude coordinates and Mars feature name. Selected downloads.
    • JMARS GIS: https://jmars.asu.edu/
      • Find interesting areas on Mars, Luna, and Earth. Record your AOI's Latitude/Longitude coordinates. Provides selected image access.
  • Find and Download Mars or Luna/Moon Orbital Images:
    • USGS Pilot: https://pilot.wr.usgs.gov/
      • Interactive Mars and Earth/Moon image locator. Select raw images, view footprints, and download. Click the bottom left “page” icon for image name.
      • Use the scene name to check for available mapped image versions at:
        • https://archives.esac.esa.int/psa/ftp/Guest-Storage-Facility/UCL-MSSL_iMars_CTX_V1.0/
        • https://hirise-pds.lpl.arizona.edu/PDS/RDR/
        • https://pds.lroc.asu.edu/data/LRO-L-LROC-5-RDR-V1.0/
    • Orbital Data Explorer (PDS Geosciences Node): https://ode.rsl.wustl.edu/
      • Select “Mars Orbital Explorer” to find Mars CTX, HiRISE, and HRSC raw (and HRSC mapped) images, and download.
      • Select “Lunar Orbital Data Explorer” to find Moon NAC and WAC raw and mapped images, and download.
